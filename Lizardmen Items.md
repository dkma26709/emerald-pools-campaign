# Lizardmen — Magic Items & Sacred Relics

Items found in Lustrian temple-ruins, claimed from defeated Skink prophets, or unearthed from spawning-pool silt. This catalogue is for use across the Emerald Pools campaign whenever a Lizardmen relic would plausibly turn up — whether the finder is Lizardmen themselves or a foe looting a fallen Skink.

Huanchi — the jaguar god of stealth, silence, and the killing strike — is the dominant thread running through this catalogue. Lustria's lost temples are thick with his glyphs, and most of what gets dug out of the jungle bears his sign. Items dedicated to other Old Ones (Sotek, Itzl, Tepok, Tzunki, and so on) appear, but they are the rarer finds.

Items are drawn from the canonical Lizardmen armoury where a clean fit exists (*Charm of the Jaguar Warrior*, *Curse-Charm of Tepok*, *Huanchi's Blessed Totem*, and so on), adapted where needed, and supplemented with new items in the same idiom where the canon is thin.

Daniel's army-specific armoury (Runtime Executioner, Async Rod, the various Patch- and Build-prefixed gear in *The Blessed of Huanchi.docx*) is a stylised reading of those same Huanchi traditions and stays exclusive to the Blessed of Huanchi.

## How Items Are Earned

Items are awarded narratively, in response to what happened on the table. The default trigger is a battle — won a scenario, slain something significant, claimed a sacred site — but one-off discoveries (Spoils of War rolls, Points of Interest) can also drop into this pool.

Items are **persistent possessions** — once claimed by a hero, they stay with that hero and are brought to every battle. Their effects trigger according to the rules printed on them: *Permanent* effects apply all the time; *One use only* effects are spent the first time they are activated, but the item otherwise stays in the hero's inventory until then; *Once per battle* effects reset between games.

Single-battle buffs (one unit gains Scouts for the next battle, +1 S on a weapon for one battle, etc.) are **not magic items** and live in the Campaign Reference's generic Reward Catalogue as consumables.

**Tier guidance:**

| Tier | When to award | Typical effect |
|------|---------------|----------------|
| **Minor Trinket** | Any battle won, or a meaningful side-objective achieved | One-use, or a small persistent effect (canonical 5–20 pts range) |
| **Standard Relic** | Decisive victory, claimed a sacred site, or slain a hero in a thematically-fitting way | Persistent, 25–50 pts of effect |
| **Legendary Relic** | Campaign milestone — pool restored, Old One ruin awakened, named relic recovered after a multi-scenario thread | Campaign-shaping, named, only one of each in the world |
| **Mythic Artefact** | The campaign's defining thread is resolved — the Emerald Pools themselves are touched, a slann-temple of the First Days awakens, or the great adversary is felled at the climax | Once per campaign only. Reshapes what the bearer's army can do. |

The table per tier lists a "Particularly fits when…" note. When an obvious thematic moment presents itself, hand the matching item directly. When nothing obvious fits, roll on the tier's table.

If the same item would be awarded twice in the campaign, treat the second drop as a duplicate fragment — either trade it in for a free re-roll on the tier table, or save it and combine two duplicates for a one-step tier upgrade (GM discretion).

Lizardmen don't strike first. Items that would break that identity (Always Strikes First and its cousins) are held back to Tier 3, where they belong. At this tier, the theme is the *patient* strike: ambush, concealment, disruption, and blows that fall harder because the foe was already unsettled.

## Tier 3 — Legendary Relics of the Old Ones

Named relics of the First Days, recovered from the deepest pools and the most ancient temples. **Each exists only once in the world** — when awarded, the item is removed from the pool and becomes a character-defining artefact. No duplicate drops, no fragments, no second copies.

Legendary relics are awarded only at genuine campaign milestones: a pool restored, a legendary foe vanquished, an Old Ones ruin awakened, or a multi-scenario thread brought to its conclusion. They are the tier where Huanchi's true gifts appear — the pouncing strike, the mask of the god, the shroud of the prowling army. ## Character Progression Charts

Each named Lizardmen character grows across **multiple independent tracks** — the signature relic, a secondary relic, and 2-3 specialty tracks that define who the character becomes over the campaign. Tracks progress independently: a character may be deep into one track and untouched in another, depending on what happens on the table.

**Relics** awaken in tiers as the campaign unfolds. Upgrades are **cumulative** (Tier N includes every effect from Tier N-1 and below). If the bearer is slain and does not recover, the relic's upgrades are lost with them. A relic cannot be traded, looted, or re-awarded. If Robbed, the relic may be reclaimed in a subsequent battle, but an enemy cannot use its awakened profile — only its mundane Base.

**Specialty tracks** grant abilities, not items — they represent the character's growing skill, reputation, and connection to Huanchi. These are lost only if the character dies permanently.

**Ascension tracks** represent the physical and hierarchical growth from hero to lord.

## Unit Blessings of Huanchi

As the campaign advances and individual units distinguish themselves on the table, they may be marked by Huanchi with a lasting blessing — a special rule earned permanently for that specific unit, carried forward to every future battle.

These are not point-buy upgrades from the army list. They are campaign-awarded, tied to the unit's deeds, and **specific to the unit that earned them** (a "Skink Cohort that earned Muffled March" is a different roster entry from another Skink Cohort that did not). Each unit type has at least two available Blessings; a unit may earn both over the campaign, but never more than one in a single scenario unless the narrative milestone clearly justifies it.

All Blessings are Huanchi-themed throughout: silent stalking, the patient strike, the jaguar's pounce, ambush from shadow, the stillness before the kill.

### Core

**Saurus Warriors**
- **First Leap** — On the turn the unit completes a successful charge, the unit gains +1 To Hit in the first round of combat.
  - *Earned when:* the unit broke an enemy unit in the first round of combat after charging.
- **Shadowed Discipline** — The unit is Immune to Psychology in the first round of every combat.
  - *Earned when:* the unit held firm against a Fear- or Terror-causing enemy and broke them in close combat.

**Skink Cohorts** *(massed Skinks, with or without Kroxigor)*
- **Muffled March** — The unit may March even when within 8" of enemy units.
  - *Earned when:* a Cohort redeployed mid-battle to catch the enemy's flank or rear at a decisive moment.
- **Huanchi's Numbers** — The unit may re-roll failed Panic tests.
  - *Earned when:* the unit held together through a horrifying moment — a friendly unit destroyed nearby, the bearer of the Huanchi-Key wounded, a Terror-causing enemy charging in.

**Skink Skirmishers**

  - *Earned when:* a Skink Skirmisher unit's flanking or scouting move directly secured a scenario objective, or the unit survived deployment-phase pre-battle effects unscathed where another would not.
- **Slip into Shadow** — After this unit completes its actions in the Shooting phase, it may immediately make a free reposition move of up to 2D6" in any direction. This move may not bring the unit into base contact with an enemy unit, and may not be used on Turn 1.

## Single-Use Boons (Once Per Campaign)

These are not battle-scoped one-use items like the Tier 1 trinkets. A Single-Use Boon is **spent on its first activation and removed from the player's inventory for the rest of the campaign**. Each represents a relic, glyph, or sacred draught burned through in a moment of crisis or triumph.

A Boon may be **re-acquired** later — through scenario rewards, Spoils of War rolls, Points of Interest exploration, or narrative events the group agrees on. The intent is that any individual Boon is rare enough to feel meaningful, but the *category* of Boons remains live across the campaign as new ones come and go.

A player may hold any number of Boons simultaneously, but may only activate one per battle unless the narrative explicitly justifies otherwise.

### The Boons

**Vial of the Spawning-Pool**
- *Use:* Once during a chosen battle, at the start of any friendly turn, nominate one Lizardmen hero. That hero immediately heals one Wound lost earlier in the battle.
- *Earned when:* a sacred pool is restored or visited during a scenario, the Huanchi-Key activates pool-water, or a Spoils of War roll thematically yields a vial of the springs.

**Glyph of the Veiled Path**
- *Use:* Before deployment in any battle, after both armies finish deploying but before any Vanguard moves, nominate one Lizardmen Infantry unit (or Skirmisher unit). That unit is removed from its deployed position and placed anywhere on the table outside the enemy's deployment zone and more than 8" from any enemy unit. The unit may not declare a charge in the first turn.
- *Earned when:* a hidden Old Ones path was uncovered in a scenario, a Skink Priest's foresight revealed a forgotten route, or a Treasure Hunt scenario was won decisively.

**Huanchi's Whispered Strike**
- *Use:* Once during a chosen battle, in any friendly Close Combat phase, nominate one enemy character within 12" of any friendly Lizardmen unit. That character immediately suffers two Strength 6 hits. Armour and Ward saves apply normally; line of sight is not required, as Huanchi's whisper finds the target through any crowd.
- *Earned when:* a Skink Priest channelled Huanchi's voice during a scenario victory, or a sacred glyph was inscribed on the effigy of a marked enemy through narrative play.

**The Stilled Heart**
- *Use:* Once during a chosen battle, the Lizardmen player may declare The Stilled Heart is being used immediately after any single die is rolled (theirs or the opponent's). That die is re-rolled, and the new result must be accepted, even if worse.
- *Earned when:* a moment of perfect stillness defined a scenario — a turn passed in silence, a Slann's intervention paused the battle, or a hero held position through impossible pressure.

**Tear of the First Spawning**
- *Use:* When a Lizardmen hero rolls on the Wounded Table after being slain in battle, the player may use the Tear to re-roll the result. The player chooses which result to keep (the original or the re-roll).
- *Earned when:* the bearer of the Huanchi-Key restored a Pool, a hero survived against impossible odds in a scenario climax, or a Lustrian shrine yielded an unbroken pool-tear.

**Echo of the Slann**
- *Use:* Once during a chosen battle, at the start of any friendly Magic phase, the Lizardmen player adds 2D6 Power Dice to their pool for that phase. These dice are added on top of the standard Winds of Magic roll, and are lost at the end of that Magic phase if unused. Additionally, the bearer's wizard gains +1 to cast for that phase only.
- *Earned when:* an Old Ones temple library was claimed in a scenario, a Slann directly channelled into a Lizardmen wizard, or the army was present at the awakening of a sacred glyph-altar.
