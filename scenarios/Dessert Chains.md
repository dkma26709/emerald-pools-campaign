# Dessert Chains

---

## Armies

**1500 points**

| Role | Player | Army |
|------|--------|------|
| Main player | Anders | Bretonnia |
| Stand-in | Hjalte | Dogs of War |

The Dogs of War player **must field a Paymaster on a Paychest**.

---

## Battlefield

A desert battlefield with a **river** running along the Dogs of War board edge. A **ship** is moored at the riverbank. Use dunes, ruins, and sparse vegetation as terrain. Standard rules apply.

---

## Deployment

Standard deployment rules.

### Prisoners

Place **3 prisoner markers** (3" templates) evenly in the Dogs of War deployment zone before deployment. Each marker represents a group of **5 prisoners**. Track each prisoner individually using dice or tokens on each marker.

---

## First Turn

The Bretonnian player goes first.

---

## Game Length

The game ends after **six turns**, or when all prisoner markers have been freed or lost.

---

## Scenario Special Rules

### Dragged to the Ship

At the start of each Dogs of War turn, for each prisoner marker, the Dogs of War player may roll a **D6 for each remaining prisoner** at that marker — but only if they have at least one **unengaged unit within 6"** of the marker.

On a **6**, that prisoner is dragged to the ship and is **lost**. Each prisoner is rolled separately.

### Free the Captives

If a Bretonnian unit is **within 6"** of a prisoner marker, it may attempt to free the prisoners in the **Close Combat phase** by rolling attacks with the models' unmodified WS and S, as if in close combat against an enemy unit. Assume the "enemy unit" has **WS 3** and **T 2**. Each wound scored frees one prisoner.

If the Bretonnian unit is also in close combat with an enemy unit, it may choose to **divert attacks** to the prisoners instead. Note that combat resolution **does not count** prisoners freed.

### Desert Heat

All units without the Immune to Psychology special rule must pass a **Leadership test** at the start of their turn or **halve their movement** that turn, as the relentless heat saps their resolve.

## Victory Conditions

### 11–15 prisoners freed: Bretonnian Major Victory

The pilgrims swear loyalty to Henry. The Bretonnian player gains **1 additional loot die** and access to **The Saved Pilgrims** Regiment of Renown:

| Profile | M | WS | BS | S | T | W | I | A | Ld | Points |
|---------|---|----|----|---|---|---|---|---|-----|--------|
| The Saved Pilgrims | 4 | 3 | 3 | 3 | 3 | 1 | 3 | 1 | 8 | 6.5 |

- **Unit size:** 20
- **Base Size:** 20x20 or 25x25mm
- **Troop Type:** Infantry
- **Equipment:** Medium armour, Polearm
- **Full command:** 20 points
- **Grail Monk upgrade:** 5 points

### 5–10 prisoners freed: Partial Victory for Bretonnia

Some were saved, but the fate of the rest weighs heavy on the knights' hearts. One of the freed pilgrims reveals himself to be a hardened warrior — **Oswin of the Fells**. Grateful for his rescue, he swears to fight alongside his saviours. He joins the army as a unique character (Sergeant-at-Arms) but **gains no experience** and **may not be equipped with magic items**.

### 1–4 prisoners freed: Dogs of War Victory

The mercenaries deliver their cargo. The pilgrims vanish downriver, never to return.

### Bonus Objective

If the Bretonnian player **kills the Paymaster**, they gain **1 additional loot die**.
