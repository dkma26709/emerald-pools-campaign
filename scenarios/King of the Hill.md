# King of the Hill

*After easily dispatching the Orcs, the Lizardmen continue their path. After some time they arrive at a high, unnatural hill, the perfect place to make camp. However, sound is coming from the other side. Somebody or something else is moving for the top.*

---

## Battlefield

The battlefield is dominated by a **large unnatural circular hill**. The hill should take up a significant portion of the centre of the table — it is THE terrain feature. Remaining terrain is placed using standard rules around the edges of the hill.

### Line of Sight on the Hill

- A unit on the **hilltop** can see the entire battlefield and has a Line of Sight value of **12**.
- For every **7"** down the slope, this value decreases by **2**.
- Units **not on the hilltop** may only draw Line of Sight within their own half of the hill.

### Shooting from High Ground

When shooting, add (or subtract) the Line of Sight difference between your unit and the target unit to your range.

## Game Length

The game ends after **six turns**.

---

## Scenario Special Rules

### High Ground

A unit is considered to have the **high ground** in close combat if its centre is closer to the closest edge of the top of the hill.

In close combat, if a unit is considered to have the high ground, it adds **+1 to its combat resolution**.

### Push and Shove

When a unit loses combat but succeeds its Break test, it is pushed backwards due to the slope:
- If the **winning unit has the high ground**, the losing unit is pushed **4"** backwards.
- Otherwise, the losing unit is pushed **2"** backwards.

The winning unit **must follow up**. This follow-up has no additional benefits (no Impact Hits, no charge bonuses, etc.).

---

## Victory Conditions

The winner is the army with the **most points on the hill** at the end of Turn 6.

### Bonus Objective

If the opposing player has **no points on the hilltop** at the end of Turn 6, the Lizardmen can sleep calmly and scout the area. In one future scenario, the Lizardmen player may **choose to go first** regardless of what the scenario specifies. This can only be used once and must be declared after deployment.
