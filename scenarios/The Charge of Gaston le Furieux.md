### The Charge of Gaston le Furieux (Bretonnia)

The Bretonnian army must field **Gaston le Furieux** as their army general. They may take **no other characters** in their army.

**Gaston le Furieux:**

- **Troop Type:** Cavalry (Human)
- **Base Size:** 25x50
- **Equipment:** Frontier Axe, Heavy Armour, Shield

**Special Rules:**

- **The Questing Vow**
- **Virtue of Audacity**
- **Audacious:** Gaston and all models with a Knightly Vow gain the Impetuous special rule. Additionally, Gaston must always issue and accept challenges.
- **Impetuous:** After charges have been declared, if any unit containing one or more models with the Impetuous special rule did not declare a charge but is within charge range of an enemy, it must take a Leadership test. If the test fails, the unit must declare a charge. If passed, it may move normally.

## Battlefield

Standard rules for battlefield terrain, with the following addition:

### The Wayside Shrine

Place a small terrain piece (shrine, chapel, or sacred marker) in the centre of the table, roughly 6" off the centre line toward the Ogre deployment zone. The shrine is an obstacle.

Fleeing peasants (3 non-combatant models) are placed within 3" of the shrine at the start of the game. They do not act, fight, or move on their own.

## Scenario Special Rules

### The Chivalric Test

The Nobleguts are fighting the very thing they aspire to be. Before the battle begins, the Ogre player must choose: charge without hesitation, or hold to the code.

**The Shrine and the Peasants:**
The peasants near the Wayside Shrine will flee toward the nearest table edge if any unit (friend or foe) moves within 6" of them. They move 2D6" at the start of each turn, directly away from the nearest unit.

- If a Bretonnian unit moves within 3" of the peasants, they are safe — removed from the table (they've been escorted to safety by the Bretonnians).
- If any unit charges through, over, or into the peasants, they are trampled — remove them as casualties.
- If the peasants reach a table edge, they escape on their own.

**The Ogre player may choose to protect the peasants** by keeping Ogre units between the peasants and the Bretonnian advance, or by steering charges away from the shrine. This is not required — it is a choice.

### Victory Conditions

**Ogre Victory:** Defeat Gaston le Furieux (removed as a casualty).
- The Ogre player gains **1 additional loot die**.

**Bretonnian Victory:** Win the battle per standard victory points without losing Gaston le Furieux.
- The Bretonnian player may mark *"Gaston le Furieux is still charging"* on their character sheet.

### Bonus Objective

If the Ogre player kills Gaston le Furieux **in a challenge**, they may forgo any loot dice and instead **claim the Frontier Axe** as a campaign trophy.

### Chivalric Bonus

If the Ogre player wins the battle AND the peasants survived (escaped or were not trampled by Ogre units), the Nobleguts earn the **Lady's First Glance** — a narrative acknowledgement that the chivalric code was honoured on the field. Record this in the Earned Rewards Log. The specific reward is agreed by the group after the battle, but it should be modest — a first thread on Hercule's surcoat, not a magic item.

If the peasants were trampled by Ogre units (even accidentally), the chivalric bonus is not earned regardless of the battle's outcome.
