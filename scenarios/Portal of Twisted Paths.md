# Portal of Twisted Paths

*With the Charnel Valley blocked, the ambitious Tomb Prince has to find another way through the World Edge Mountains. The options are few, and none of them appealing. Wading through endless tunnels below the mountains seems like a surefire way to get lost. Trying the valley again is likely suicidal. Going around the mountain chain would take far too long. Crossing the higher peaks would be nigh impossible. The last option is further agitating the lizardmen by using their technology to reactivate the ancient portal of Xzachuatl, which has lain dormant for untold millennia. With no way around it, the Tomb Prince dispatches his armies to claim the sacred grounds and get to work while awaiting the inevitable assault. It shouldn't take long.*

**1500 points each**

|------|--------|------|

**First direct clash between two campaign armies.** Both players' heroes are at real risk — injuries, permanent debuffs, and narrative consequences are on the table.

## Battlefield

At the centre of the battlefield sits the **dormant portal** on a hill. At each end of the Tomb Kings deployment zone is placed a **Battery Charging Station**.

- **Forests** are scattered across the battlefield **except** in the Tomb Kings deployment zone.
- A few **hills** are placed in the Tomb Kings deployment zone.

---

## Deployment

Both players take turns deploying their army within **12"** of their table edge. Roll off to see who deploys first.

---

## First Turn

Roll to see who goes first. The player who finished deploying first gets **+1** to their roll.

---

## Game Length

The game ends after **seven turns**, or when every Tomb King Hero has entered the portal.

---

## Scenario Special Rules

### Portal Power

The portal begins **dormant** and counts as an **obstacle** (units cannot move through it).

To open the portal, the Tomb King player must charge it:
- At the **end of every round**, if the Tomb King player has a unit within **6"** of a Charging Station, they add **+1** to the portal's Charging Counter for each station controlled.
- When the Charging Counter reaches **6**, the **portal opens**.

Once open, a unit enters the portal by **moving into base contact** with it.

### Charging Stations

There are **two** Charging Stations, one at each end of the Tomb Kings deployment zone. The Tomb Kings control a station if they have at least one unit within 6" of it with no enemy units within 6".

The Lizardmen can contest a station by moving a unit within 6" — a contested station does not add to the counter.

---

## Victory Conditions

### Tomb Kings Victory

Get at least **one hero through the portal**. For every hero after the first to get through, the Tomb Kings gain **+1 loot die**.

Ideally both the Tomb Prince and all of his retinue should get through the portal.

### Lizardmen Victory

Prevent the portal from opening, or prevent all Tomb King heroes from entering it if it opens.

---

## Campaign Consequences

### If the Tomb Kings win:

- The Tomb Kings are through — closer to the Pools, but in unfamiliar territory. The next TK scenario involves survival and exploration on the other side.
- The portal does not close cleanly. It remains **unstable**. This has consequences beyond this scenario (see Daniel's campaign notes).
- The Lizardmen failed to hold a sacred Old Ones site. Narrative weight.

### If the Lizardmen win:

- The portal is held. The Tomb Kings must find another route — the golden tablets may describe one.
- The sacred site is intact. The Lizardmen maintain control of the Old Ones' infrastructure in this region.
