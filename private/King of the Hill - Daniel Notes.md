# King of the Hill — Daniel's Notes

**These notes are for Daniel only. Do not share with other players.**

---

## The Hill is the Device

The hilltop is not merely stone. Beneath the overgrowth, the entire hill is an **Old Ones construct** — a node in the geomantic web, dormant for millennia. The "unnatural" shape described in the scenario text is literal: the Old Ones built it.

If the Lizardmen hold the hilltop at the end of the game, Skrr'ipt reads the glyphs carved into the exposed stone at the summit. The device is partially activated.

The device is part of the **same network as the Portal of Twisted Paths** — activating it grants the Lizardmen advance understanding of how the portal functions and how the web connects these sites.

## Campaign Reward

If the Lizardmen win and hold the hilltop:

In the **Portal of Twisted Paths** scenario, the Lizardmen player may **place one additional terrain piece** after normal terrain setup, before deployment begins. This represents the Old Ones' infrastructure partially defending itself.

If the Lizardmen lose or do not hold the hilltop at game end, the Portal scenario plays straight — no bonus. The device remains dormant but may be revisited in a future scenario.

## What the Group Sees

The other players see a standard King of the Hill scenario with interesting terrain rules. The "unnatural" hill is flavour text to them. Only after the battle (if the Lizardmen win) does the discovery become visible — Skrr'ipt examining the summit, finding glyphs, the group learning that this was built, not grown.

The terrain manipulation reward for the Portal scenario can be revealed at that point — it's a visible campaign consequence, not a hidden one. What stays hidden is the geomantic web context and why the device matters beyond this one bonus.
