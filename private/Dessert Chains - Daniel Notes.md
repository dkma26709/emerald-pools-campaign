# Dessert Chains — Daniel's Notes

**These notes are for Daniel only. Do not share with other players.**

---

## The Witch Hunter

One of the three prisoner groups contains **a Witch Hunter of the Empire** among its captives. On the table, he is not identified separately — he is simply one of the 5 prisoners in that group. Assign him to the centre group, or randomise before deployment.

The other players do not need to know he exists until he's revealed narratively — either when the prisoners are freed ("one of them isn't a pilgrim...") or after the battle.

### His Mission

The Witch Hunter was sent by the Empire to investigate **dark energy readings** emanating from the region — the same source the Lizardmen sealed at the underworld entrance in Scenario 7. He was captured by the slavers en route.

From the Lizardmen's perspective: the source is sealed. From the Witch Hunter's perspective: he never reached the site and doesn't know what happened. His intelligence is stale — but he doesn't know that yet.

### Survival Conditions

The Witch Hunter's fate is determined by how many prisoners from his group are freed:

| Result | Witch Hunter status |
|--------|-------------------|
| 3+ prisoners freed from his group | **Alive.** In Bretonnian hands. Full intelligence and some retinue intact. |
| 1-2 prisoners freed from his group | **Alive but wounded.** Alone, records lost. Fragments of intelligence only. |
| 0 prisoners freed (group lost to ship) | **Lost.** Dead or shipped off. The Empire introduction thread is delayed. |

### Empire Introduction

The Witch Hunter's rescue is the hook for Anders' Empire army. After Dessert Chains:

- **If alive:** The Witch Hunter reports to the Empire. Anders' army arrives following up on the intelligence. The scenario quality depends on how much of his retinue survived.
- **If lost:** Anders needs a different reason to bring the Empire. The thread isn't dead — the Empire sent an agent and he never reported back. That's its own hook, just a darker one.

### The Dormant Thread

The Witch Hunter's mandate appears resolved — the Lizardmen sealed the source. His mission seems pointless.

**The mandate reactivates if the geomantic web is stressed** — most likely by the Tomb Kings forcing the Portal of Twisted Paths. When the web destabilises, the dark energy readings return. The Witch Hunter's "stale" intelligence becomes current again, and the Empire has a live crisis on its hands.

This is the connection between Dessert Chains and the Portal scenario. The Witch Hunter is the bridge between the Bretonnian legacy and the Empire introduction, and the geomantic web is the trigger that makes him relevant.

### What to Reveal and When

- **During/after Dessert Chains:** Reveal the Witch Hunter to the group when the prisoners are freed. "One of them is an Empire agent." The group learns he was sent to investigate something, but the details stay vague.
- **After Portal of Twisted Paths (if TK win):** The Witch Hunter's instruments start giving readings again. Now the group understands why the Empire is coming.
- **If the web isn't stressed:** The Witch Hunter stays a background character. The Empire still arrives (Anders needs to play), but the Witch Hunter's mission is a footnote, not a driving thread.
