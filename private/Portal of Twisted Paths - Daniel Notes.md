# Portal of Twisted Paths — Daniel's Notes

**These notes are for Daniel only. Do not share with other players.**

---

## The Geomantic Web

The portal of Xzachuatl is a **node in the geomantic web** — the Old Ones' planetary infrastructure that connects spawning pools, temple-cities, and sacred sites. Only the Slann truly understand it. Skrr'ipt has fragments of understanding through his connection to Xhol'Taun the Veiled and the Huanchi-Key.

The Tomb Kings see a doorway. They do not know it is load-bearing.

### If the Tomb Kings force the portal open:

The portal activates but does not close cleanly. The forced activation sends stress through the geomantic web:

- **The Scenario 7 seal weakens.** Not immediately — over time. The underworld entrance the Lizardmen sealed begins to show signs of activity. This is a slow-burn thread, not an instant break.
- **Glyph-markers across the region flicker.** Skrr'ipt feels it through the Huanchi-Key. The chatter gets louder — or goes silent, which is worse.
- **The Witch Hunter's instruments reactivate** (if he was rescued in Dessert Chains). The dark energy readings he was sent to investigate are back. His dormant mandate activates.
- **The Pools themselves may be affected.** If the Pools are a structural keystone (as suspected), web stress could change what happens when the armies finally reach them.

None of this is visible to the Tomb Kings player. He won — he got through. The consequences are happening in systems he can't see.

### If the Lizardmen hold the portal:

- The web remains intact. No cascade.
- The Witch Hunter's thread stays dormant.
- The Tomb Kings must find another route — the golden tablets may describe one, but that route may have its own problems.

## The Hill-Device Bonus

If **King of the Hill** was won and the hilltop held, the hill-device (part of the same geomantic network) was partially activated by Skrr'ipt.

In this scenario, the Lizardmen player may **place one additional terrain piece** after normal terrain setup — before deployment begins. This represents the Old Ones' infrastructure partially defending itself. Place it anywhere on the table following normal terrain placement rules.

If King of the Hill was lost or the hilltop was not held, this bonus does not apply.

## Skrr'ipt's Role

Skrr'ipt is the key to understanding what's happening. If he is on the battlefield:

- He can **feel** the portal powering up through the Huanchi-Key. This is narrative — no mechanical effect, but it informs Daniel's tactical decisions. The closer the Charging Counter gets to 6, the more agitated the Key becomes.
- If the portal opens, Skrr'ipt knows the web has been stressed. He won't know the full extent until after the battle, but the immediate sensation is clear: something shifted that shouldn't have.
- If Skrr'ipt is killed during this scenario, the Lizardmen lose their only direct link to the web's status. The consequences still happen, but the army won't understand what's happening until the effects become visible (the seal cracking, the glyph-markers going dark).

## Strategic Considerations

The Lizardmen have two objectives in this scenario:
1. **Visible:** Prevent the Tomb Kings from getting through the portal.
2. **Hidden:** Prevent the portal from being forced at all — even if the TK don't get through, charging the portal stresses the web. Every turn the Charging Counter advances is damage being done.

This means **contesting the Charging Stations is as important as holding the portal itself**. If the TK charge the portal to 5 but never open it (counter stuck at 5), the web was still stressed — just not as badly. The ideal outcome is keeping the counter as low as possible, not just preventing it from reaching 6.

Consider whether to communicate this to the group after the battle. If the portal was forced, the Lizardmen have a reason to explain (in character) that something went wrong with the site. This plants the seed for the web-stress arc without revealing the full picture.
