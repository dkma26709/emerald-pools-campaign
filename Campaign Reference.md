# The Emerald Pools — Campaign Reference

---

## Campaign Overview

Four armies search for the Emerald Pools — an ancient Lizardmen site of immense power, lost to the jungle for millennia. Each faction has their own reasons for seeking them. The campaign is vibes-based: scenarios and consequences are decided collaboratively between sessions based on what happened last time.

### The Trail

Each faction tracks **Trail Progress** (starting at 0). This represents how close they are to locating and reaching the Emerald Pools. Trail is gained through scenario victories, bonus objectives, and narrative events. When the campaign reaches its climax, Trail Progress determines deployment advantages and narrative positioning in the finale.

Trail is a guide, not a straitjacket — adjust it as the narrative demands.

### Relationship to "Springs of Eternal Life"

This campaign evolved from the earlier "Search for the Springs of Eternal Life" system. That system's core rules (XP, wounded table, spoils of war) remain the mechanical foundation. The shift toward "vibes-based" means:
- Scenarios and consequences are decided collaboratively, not pre-scripted
- Rewards can be agreed upon after a battle rather than only rolled for
- The Trail replaces rigid campaign progression
- Faction-specific Points of Interest can still be used but aren't mandatory

The full original rules are documented in "Springs of Eternal Life.rtf" on the Desktop. Key systems summarised below.

### Army Transitions

Some players are switching armies during the campaign. Early scenarios may feature different factions than the ones listed above. How to handle this narratively:

- **Orcs & Goblins → Ogre Kingdoms:** The Orcs were drawn to the region by the fighting but lacked the staying power or purpose to keep searching. The Ogres arrive later, drawn by hunger and the Great Maw's call. Any Trail Progress or narrative consequences earned by the O&G can either transfer to the Ogres (they stumbled onto the greenskins' trail) or be wiped clean (fresh start). Agree between players.
- **Bretonnia → Empire:** The Bretonnian expedition was a scouting force or crusading party. When the Empire arrives, they may be following up on the Bretonnians' reports, or the Bretonnians simply withdrew and an Imperial force replaced them. Again — Trail and consequences can transfer or reset, as agreed.

### Stand-In Armies

Not every battle is fought between players' main campaign armies. Players sometimes run **stand-in factions** for variety or because the scenario calls for it. The narrative still moves the plot forward — stand-in battles are canon. The only difference is that the stand-in player's persistent campaign heroes aren't at personal risk (no XP, wounds, or spoils for that player's main army). Note which army is a stand-in in the session log where relevant.

#### King of the Hill
**Lizardmen (Daniel, campaign) vs TBD (Jesper, stand-in)** — 1600pts — Planned

*Source: `King of the Hill.docx`*

A large unnatural circular hill dominates the battlefield. Detailed hill rules: LoS 12 from hilltop (decreases by 2 per 7" downslope), off-hill units see own half only, **High Ground** (+1 CR if centre closer to hilltop), **Push and Shove** (losers pushed 4" back if winner has high ground, 2" otherwise — winner must follow), shooting range modified by LoS difference.

- **Bonus objective:** Opponent has 0 points on hilltop at end of T6 → choose first turn in one future scenario.

#### Dessert Chains
**Bretonnia (Anders, campaign) vs Dogs of War (Hjalte, stand-in)** — 1500pts — Planned

*Source: `Dessert Chains.docx`*

Anders' last Bretonnian scenario. Desert battlefield with a river along the DoW board edge and a ship moored at the riverbank. DoW must field a Paymaster on Paychest. 3 prisoner markers (3", 5 prisoners each, tracked individually) in DoW deployment zone. **Dragged to Ship:** each DoW turn, D6 per prisoner per marker (if DoW unit within 6"), 6 = prisoner lost. **Free the Captives:** Bretonnian unit within 6" attacks prisoners in CC phase (WS3/T2 target). **Desert Heat:** non-ITP units test Ld or half movement.

- **Victory scaling:** 11-15 freed = Major Victory + loot die + **The Saved Pilgrims** RoR (M4/WS3/BS3/S3/T3/W1/I3/A1/Ld8, 6.5pts, medium armour + polearm, unit size 20). 5-10 freed = Partial + **Oswin of the Fells** unique character (Sergeant-at-Arms, no XP, no magic items). 1-4 freed = DoW Victory.
- **Bonus:** Kill Paymaster = extra loot die.
- **First turn:** Bretonnia goes first. 6 turns.
- **CAMPAIGN ADDITION — The Witch Hunter:** One prisoner group contains a Witch Hunter of the Empire, sent to investigate dark energy from the region (same source sealed in Scenario 7). His survival determines the quality of Anders' Empire introduction. His mandate is dormant — reactivates if the geomantic web is stressed.
- **Map:** `scenarios/dessert-chains.json`.

#### Portal of Twisted Paths
**Tomb Kings (Hjalte, campaign) vs Lizardmen (Daniel, campaign)** — 1500pts each — Planned

*Source: `Portal of Twisted Paths - Censored.docx` (Hjalte's version — Daniel's version has hidden stakes below)*

First direct clash between campaign armies. Portal on a central hill, two **Battery Charging Stations** at each end of the TK deployment zone. **Portal Power:** each turn, +1 to charge counter per station controlled (TK unit within 6"). Counter reaches 6 = portal opens. Until opened, the portal is an obstacle. Forests scattered across battlefield (not in TK zone), hills in TK zone. 12" deployment, roll for first turn, 7 turns or when all TK heroes enter portal.

- **TK objective:** Get at least one hero through the portal = win. Each additional hero = +1 loot die.
- **Lizardmen objective:** Prevent the portal from opening, or prevent TK heroes from entering it if it opens.
- **HIDDEN (Daniel only) — Geomantic web stress:** The portal is a node in the geomantic web. The TK don't know this. Forcing it open won't break anything immediately, but it sets things in motion — the Scenario 7 seal weakens over time, the Witch Hunter's readings reactivate, glyph-markers flicker. The TK are the catalyst, not the villain. Only the Lizardmen (through Skrr'ipt) can sense the damage.
- **If TK win:** Heroes through. Portal stays unstable. Web stress begins. TK are closer to the Pools but exposed.
- **If Lizardmen win:** Network intact. TK must find another route (golden tablets may describe one). Witch Hunter thread stays dormant.
- **HIDDEN — Hill-device bonus:** If King of the Hill was won and the device activated, the Lizardmen player may place 1 additional terrain piece after normal setup.
- **Map:** `scenarios/portal-of-twisted-paths.json`.

## Army: Ogre Kingdoms — The Nobleguts

*Full faction documentation lives in `Nobleguts.md` — magic items, character progression charts, unit blessings, regiments of renown, and the campaign-unlock units. Summary below.*

### Motivation

The **Nobleguts** (formerly the Gorgerguts) were knighted in Bretonnia by a band of dying questing knights they had been paid to protect. ## Core Campaign Rules (from "Springs of Eternal Life")

### Army Progression

Armies start small and grow as the campaign unfolds. Access to unit types, character mounts, and army size expands through play — not all at once, but earned through the narrative.

**Starting Restrictions:**
- **Heroes only** — no Lord choices at campaign start. Lords become available when a character reaches Ascension Tier 3 in their progression chart.
- **Core + Special units** — full access from the start.
- **No Rare units** — each Rare unit type has its own unlock condition, earned through scenarios.
- **Character mounts** — basic mounts available from the start (Cold One, Mournfang). More powerful mounts (Carnosaur, Stonehorn, Grimhorn Rhinox) must be unlocked.
- **Campaign-unlock units** — locked behind their specific narrative triggers (Ogre Knights after the Lady's Blessing, Chameleon Stalkers after Pool restoration, etc.).

**Points Growth:**
Army points grow as more scenarios are played. The group agrees on a starting base and increment — a guideline, not a rigid formula. Adjust as the narrative demands.

**Rare Unit Unlocks:**
Each Rare unit has its own unlock condition tied to the faction's story. These are **suggestions** — the actual trigger should be woven into scenarios as they arise rather than pre-determined. Bigger monsters (Dread Saurian, Ice Mammoth, Stonehorn) should be the result of multi-scenario threads, not single-battle rewards.

Unlocks are **per faction** — each army earns its own access independently.

### Character Injury System

When a named campaign hero is taken out of action — slain in battle, captured, crushed by a scenario event, or anything else the group agrees counts — the group picks an injury from that character's specific list. The trigger doesn't have to be "removed as a casualty" — a dramatic narrative moment, a failed objective, or a scenario consequence can all inflict injuries if the group agrees it makes sense.

**Severity levels:**

| Level | Meaning | When to inflict |
|-------|---------|-----------------|
| **Temporary** | Lasts 1–2 battles, then fades. Represents bruises, shaken confidence, disrupted abilities | Default for most casualties. A character's first few injuries should almost always be temporary |
| **Permanent** | Stays forever. Strips a defining ability or reduces a core stat. Changes who the character is | After a character has suffered 3+ temporary injuries across the campaign, or when the moment on the table demands it — a crushing defeat, a challenge lost badly, a dramatic failure |
| **Death** | The character is gone. Their relic, their track progress, their story — ended | After 2+ permanent injuries, death is on the table. The group decides together. Death should be *earned* by the narrative, not random — a last stand, a sacrifice, a duel they couldn't win. One more permanent injury after death becomes possible can substitute if the group prefers slow decline over a final moment |

These are guidelines, not formulae. A character's first casualty might be permanent if the moment calls for it (a lance through the chest is not a bruise). A character with three permanent injuries might survive another battle if the story isn't ready to let them go. The table is the authority — the list below is a menu of options.

**Recovering from temporary injuries:** Temporary injuries fade automatically after the stated duration (usually 1–2 battles). A particularly good narrative moment — a Healing Draught, a Pool restoration, Pascal's cooking, a rest between campaigns — can clear one early.

**Recovering from permanent injuries:** Permanent injuries do not heal on their own. A major campaign event — a Pool restoration, the Grail, a divine intervention — *could* heal one if the group agrees, but this should be rare and meaningful. Permanent means permanent unless something extraordinary happens.

**When a character dies:**
- Their relic upgrades are lost with them (the weapon reverts to Base if anyone picks it up)
- Their progression track progress is gone
- Consequences ripple to bonded characters (noted per character below)
- The army loses access to that hero slot unless a replacement is narratively introduced

### Reward Power Guidelines

To keep things balanced in a long campaign:

- **Permanent stat increases** (+1 WS, +1 S, etc.) — rare, earned through multi-battle objectives or climactic moments
- **Permanent special rules** (Fear, Killing Blow, Stubborn) — earned through character-defining moments
- **One-battle buffs** — earned through single scenario victories or bonus objectives
- **Consumables** — found via exploration, treasure hunts, or agreed between sessions
- **Injuries** — should balance rewards; a hero who's gained a lot should occasionally suffer setbacks. Named heroes use the Character Injury System above (faction-specific injury lists). Other heroes: agree narratively or use the Advancement Table in reverse as inspiration.
