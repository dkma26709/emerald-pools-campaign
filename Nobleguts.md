# The Nobleguts — Ogre Kingdoms

## Tribe Background

Ogre tribes have wandered across the world for thousands of years in search of plunder and food. One such tribe, the **Gorgerguts**, was wandering through Bretonnia when they happened upon a small temple dedicated to the Lady of the Lake. The temple was guarded by a handful of old and weary questing knights, who had heard that a roaming band of beastmen were passing through this area and had decided to make their final stand here. The knights, knowing that the fight was hopeless, saw the arrival of the ogres as a sign from the Lady and asked them for assistance.

The ogre chieftain, Brogg, required payment, but the knights had nothing to offer. In a desperate plea, the leader of the knights offered to grant the chieftain a knighthood. Brogg, initially hesitant, was ultimately persuaded when the knights explained the rights that would be bestowed upon Brogg and his tribe — they would be considered of high social status and would thus be able to demand food from the local peasants.

Brogg knelt to be knighted, but as he swore his oath to the Lady and to protect the innocent, something struck a chord in him. He felt a wave of elation, and for the briefest of moments he even felt his ever-gnawing hunger diminish just slightly. When he rose, he had a new purpose.

When the beastmen arrived, they found an ogre tribe eager for battle, and the mutants were trampled under a stampede of flesh and jagged iron. Afterwards, Brogg spent a few days talking with the knights, learning everything he could about their customs and sacred mission. When a week had passed, Brogg had sworn a new vow and taken a new name. Henceforth, he would be known as the questing knight **Hercule Grailseeker**, and his tribe would be the **Nobleguts**. Setting out into the world, he would try to uphold the virtues of a true knight (as well as he could remember them) and ultimately earn the Favour of the Lady and be allowed to drink from the Grail.

- Ogre Charge
- Immunity (Poison Attacks)

**Narrative:**
Pascal was always a strange ogre. He had been part of another tribe that travelled to Bretonnia and had taken to the local cuisine, but he found that his former tribe did not appreciate his new menu. He was kicked out and found himself alone in a strange world. By chance he happened upon Hercule and his band, and Hercule was more than happy to take him in.

Since then, Pascal — often called Pastry — has elevated his cooking skills to a new level, to the point that the rare outsider is even willing to join in the feast of the Nobleguts (though most visitors would be wise to leave early lest they themselves become dinner). Pascal usually calls himself a *chef* rather than a butcher, though his tribe tends to default to "baker". This is further solidified by the fact that Pascal goes into battle wielding a baguette that he has hardened to the point that it is strong as steel — and only an ogre would be able to digest it. ## Nobleguts Armoury — Chivalric Relics & Sacred Cookware

Items acquired through feats of arms, culinary achievements, or the Lady's dubious favour. This catalogue covers the campaign-specific items available to the Nobleguts — whether found on a defeated knight, blessed (allegedly) by a passing damsel, or pulled from Pascal's kitchen stores.

The Lady of the Lake is the dominant thread here. Whether she has actually noticed the Nobleguts is a matter of theological debate, but the tribe believes she has, and that belief makes them dangerous. Beneath the chivalric veneer, the Great Maw's hunger still rumbles — most Ogres have suppressed it, but it surfaces in the darker items and in Ogres who have not fully embraced Hercule's vision.

Items from the canonical Ogre Kingdoms armoury exist alongside these — the Nobleguts-specific catalogue represents the tribe's unique identity, not a replacement for the army list's standard gear.

### How Items Are Earned

Items are awarded narratively, in response to what happened on the table. The default trigger is a battle — a chivalric deed performed, a foe vanquished honourably (or at least with the appearance of honour), a sacred site defended, or a feast of particular excellence. Spoils of War rolls and Points of Interest can also yield items from this catalogue.

Items are **persistent possessions** — once claimed by a hero, they stay with that hero and are brought to every battle. *Permanent* effects apply always; *One use only* effects are spent the first time activated but the item stays in the hero's inventory until then; *Once per battle* effects reset between games.

Single-battle buffs (one unit gains Stubborn for the next battle, +1 S for one battle, etc.) are **not magic items** and live in the Campaign Reference's generic Reward Catalogue as consumables.

**Tier guidance:**

| Tier | When to award | Typical effect |
|------|---------------|----------------|
| **Minor Token** | Any battle won, or a minor chivalric deed (defended the weak, held the line, shared rations with a Gnoblar) | One-use, or a small persistent effect (5–20 pts range) |
| **Virtue's Reward** | Decisive victory, a genuine act of knightly virtue, or a named foe defeated in single combat | Persistent, 25–50 pts of effect |
| **Lady's Gift** | Campaign milestone — the Lady appears to actually respond, or a feat of arms that echoes the Grail stories. The real ones. | Campaign-shaping, named. Only one of each in the world |
| **Grail Relic** | The campaign's defining thread resolves — the Lady grants her favour directly, or the Nobleguts prove themselves worthy at the climax | Once per campaign only. Reshapes what the bearer's army can do |

When an obvious thematic moment presents itself, hand the matching item directly. When nothing obvious fits, roll on the tier's table.

If the same item would be awarded twice, treat the second drop as a duplicate — either trade it in for a free re-roll on the tier table, or save two duplicates for a one-step tier upgrade.

### Tier 2 — Virtue's Rewards

Proper chivalric artefacts — oaths made steel, the Lady's alleged blessings, and one or two pieces of Pascal's kitchen arsenal that have proven their worth beyond the campfire. Items at this tier are persistent, sit in the 25–50 pts equivalent range, and change how a hero or unit fights.

The Nobleguts don't understand chivalry as Bretonnians do. What they understand is *conviction*. A Bretonnian knight charges because his code demands it — a Noblegut charges because he has sworn to, and an Ogre who has sworn to do something does it with his full weight behind it. Items at this tier reward that conviction: the lance lowered in earnest, the challenge issued without hesitation, the line held because a promise was made.

### Tier 3 — The Lady's Gifts

Named relics that mark the Lady's genuine attention — or so the Nobleguts believe. **Each exists only once in the world.** When awarded, the item is removed from the pool and becomes a character-defining artefact. No duplicates, no fragments, no second copies.

These are not trinkets worn for luck or oaths scratched on stones. At this tier, something has answered. Whether it is the Lady of the Lake, the Great Maw's echo twisted into an unfamiliar shape, or sheer conviction given physical weight — the Nobleguts do not ask, and the items do not explain. They simply work in ways that Minor Tokens and Virtue's Rewards do not.

Awarded only at genuine campaign milestones: a feat of arms that would move a Bretonnian court, a sacred site defended against overwhelming odds, a named enemy felled in honourable combat after a quest to find them, or a moment where the tribe's chivalric code was tested to breaking and held.

### Tier 4 — Grail Relics

These do not belong to the Nobleguts. They belong to whatever answered Hercule's prayer at the temple — whatever stirred in the fountain when he knelt, whatever made the flowers grow on a Gnoblar's grave-picked crown. A Grail Relic appears only when the campaign's defining question is answered: has the Lady truly chosen the Nobleguts, or have they been chasing a beautiful lie?

**Only one Grail Relic may exist in the world across the entire Nobleguts campaign.** If the narrative would award a second, the group either reshapes it as a Tier 3 Lady's Gift, or holds the pool closed entirely.

## Character Progression Charts

Each named Nobleguts character grows across **multiple independent tracks**. Tracks progress independently — a character may be deep into one track and untouched in another, depending on what happens on the table.

**Relics** awaken in tiers. Upgrades are **cumulative**. If the bearer is slain and does not recover, relic upgrades are lost with them. A relic cannot be traded or looted for use.

**Specialty tracks** grant abilities, not items — the character's growing reputation, conviction, and command. Lost only on permanent death.

**Ascension tracks** represent the growth from hero to lord.

## Knightly Honours

As the campaign advances and individual units distinguish themselves, Hercule bestows upon them a **Knightly Honour** — a title, a token, or a rite that the unit carries forward into every future battle. The tribe takes these seriously. An Ogre Bull who has earned "The Duke's Advance" fights differently from one who has not — not because of the title, but because of what he did to earn it, and what the tribe will expect him to do again.

These are not point-buy upgrades. They are campaign-awarded, tied to the unit's deeds, and **specific to the unit that earned them**. Each unit type has two available Honours; a unit may earn both over the campaign, but never more than one in a single scenario unless the narrative clearly justifies it.

### Core

**Ogre Bulls**
- **The Duke's Advance** — On the turn the unit completes a successful charge, the unit's Ogre Charge Impact Hits gain +1 Strength.
  - *Earned when:* the unit's charge broke a ranked enemy formation in the first round of combat — the Ogres hit like the lance-charge Hercule dreams of.
- **Virtue of the Line** — The unit is Immune to Panic while within 12" of the army General.
  - *Earned when:* the unit held the battle line against a Fear- or Terror-causing enemy and did not break. The Duke noticed.

**Ironguts**

  - *Earned when:* the unit's armour was the difference between holding and breaking — they endured a sustained assault that would have shattered lighter troops.
- **The Last Stand** — The unit gains Stubborn while within 6" of a scenario objective or the army's deployment zone.
  - *Earned when:* the unit held ground around a key position against multiple enemy charges without giving an inch.

## Single-Use Boons (Once Per Campaign)

These are not battle-scoped one-use items like the Tier 1 tokens. A Single-Use Boon is **spent on its first activation and removed from the player's inventory for the rest of the campaign**. Each represents a blessing, a feast, or a moment of faith burned through in a single act of crisis or triumph.

A Boon may be **re-acquired** later — through scenario rewards, Spoils of War rolls, Points of Interest exploration, or narrative events the group agrees on. Any individual Boon is rare enough to feel meaningful, but the *category* of Boons remains live across the campaign.

A player may hold any number of Boons simultaneously, but may only activate one per battle unless the narrative explicitly justifies otherwise.

### The Boons

**Pascal's Grand Feast**
- *Use:* Before deployment in any battle, Pascal prepares a feast of unusual quality. Nominate one friendly Ogre unit. That unit gains +1 Toughness for the entire first game turn, and the first time that unit completes a successful charge during the battle, its Ogre Charge Impact Hits gain +1 Strength.
- *Earned when:* Pascal cooks something extraordinary during a scenario — rare ingredients found, a beast slain whose meat has unusual properties, or a feast prepared under fire that the tribe remembers long after.

**The Lady's Whisper**
- *Use:* Once during a chosen battle, at the start of any friendly turn, nominate one friendly Ogre character. That character may immediately re-roll one die — any single die rolled this turn (To Hit, To Wound, armour save, charge distance, or Leadership test). The re-roll must be accepted, even if worse.
- *Earned when:* a moment of genuine faith — a hero prayed (in their fashion) before a battle and something went right that shouldn't have, or a scenario was won by a margin so thin it felt like intervention.

**The Questing Beast's Trail**
- *Use:* Before deployment in any battle, after both armies finish deploying but before Vanguard moves, nominate one friendly Ogre Cavalry or Monstrous Cavalry unit. That unit is removed from its deployed position and placed anywhere on the table outside the enemy's deployment zone and more than 10" from any enemy unit. The unit may not declare a charge in the first turn. The trail was already blazed — the Lancers merely followed it.
- *Earned when:* a hidden path was discovered during a scenario, a scouting victory revealed the enemy's flank, or the Sabretusks found a route no one else could see.

**Hercule's Oath of Vengeance**
- *Use:* Once during a chosen battle, after a friendly Ogre character is slain or after the army General loses a challenge, the Nobleguts player may declare the Oath. All friendly Ogre units on the battlefield immediately gain +1 To Hit in close combat until the end of the following turn. The tribe swore. The tribe remembers.
- *Earned when:* an Ogre hero was slain in a previous scenario and the tribe vowed revenge, or a defeat was suffered that the group agrees demands an answer.
