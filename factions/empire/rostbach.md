## Background

Born in the shadow of Ofenstadt's furnaces. Son of a smith and grandson of a miner — soot in his blood, iron in his faith. Now marches far from those furnaces, charged by the Counsel with leading the expedition south to find the missing merchant Aldric Brunnholt.

## Concept — Direction Sketch

*First-pass design direction, pending Anders feedback. Not committed mechanics.*

### Identity

Friedrich Rostbach is not a cathedral priest. His faith was forged in Ofenstadt's furnaces — built from soot, iron, and the conviction that hammering things until they're true is also how Sigmar works. He leads the expedition because he's the man the Counsel trusts not to break: stubborn, grounded, slow to anger. The Land of the Dead is exactly the wrong place for him — desiccated cold instead of furnace-heat — and that's the point. Iron faith forged in one furnace, having to prove it burns anywhere.

He is **front-rank**: in the unit, swinging his hammer, Sigmar's instrument as much as Sigmar's mouthpiece.

### The Ofenstadt-Sigmarite Cult

Ofenstadt's faith is a furnace-cult variation on Sigmar — not the cathedral religion. Forge-priests rather than war-priests. Sigmar is the smith who hammered the Empire into shape; their reverence runs through anvil and bellows rather than altar and chalice. Rostbach embodies it.

### Naming Palette

Sigmarite faith + smithcraft + German flavour. *Schmiede* (forge), *Glut* (embers), *Funke* (spark), *Eisenglaube* (iron faith). Tier names can read as forging stages — Ignite → Temper → Anneal → Hammer → Quench. Item names lean to **Forge-Sworn**, **Ofenheim's Hammer**, **The Twin-Tailed Anvil**, **Embers of the First Furnace**.

### Proposed Tracks (4)

1. **The Hammer (5 tiers)** — *the priest's weapon*
   His warhammer, forged from Ofenstadt iron, blessed at the cathedral but tempered in his city's fires. Doubles as Sigmarite icon. Themes: smite undead, single-decisive-blow effects (sparingly), faith-fuelled re-rolls, Initiative bonuses when wrath is up. Unlocks tied to scenario beats — first kill of a named undead character, holding the line through crisis.

2. **The Iron Faith (3 tiers)** — *the prayers*
   His Battle Prayers of Sigmar expand as his faith proves itself. Higher tiers add Ofenstadt-specific prayers (forge-flavoured) or extend an existing prayer's reach. *Unit-supportive* — the prayers harden the men around him, not Rostbach himself. Sample directions: a "Bellows of Sigmar" prayer (unit re-rolls failed To-Wound), an "Anvil's Stance" prayer (unit Stubborn one phase).

3. **The Counsel's Burden (3 levels)** — *the expedition leader*
   He didn't ask for command — he took it because the Counsel asked. As the expedition survives, he grows from priest into something heavier: a banner, the man whose presence steadies the column. Themes: army-wide Ld bonuses, rally improvements, Inspiring-Presence-like effects on Ofenstadt veterans.

4. **Ascension: Arch Lector (3 tiers)** — Warrior Priest → Arch Lector
   Standard shape. Tier 1: +1 W or +1 Ld. Tier 2: faith pool / additional prayer / Stubborn aura. Tier 3: Lord choice — Arch Lector of Sigmar, may take the War Altar, full prayer access.

### The Witch Hunter Weave — *The Inquisitor's Mantle*

Scenario 5's outcome sets a *conditional fifth thread*:

- **Inquisitor's Brief** (Witch Hunter rescued whole) → Rostbach has a partner-in-faith in the field. The Iron Faith gains a tier for shared prayers. He stays a priest.
- **Inquisitor's Warning** (Witch Hunter wounded, retinue dead) → Rostbach inherits half-finished work. The Iron Faith gains a tier granting *Hatred (Undead)*. The mantle is half on him.
- **Missing Agent** (Witch Hunter lost) → A new conditional track activates: **The Inquisitor's Mantle (3 tiers)** — Hatred Undead, dispel re-rolls vs Lore of Death, a purifying-fire prayer. The priest becomes the inquisitor because there's no one else.

The Mantle should *cost* something narratively. Iron faith inherits a shadow it didn't ask for. Could pair with a small downside — losing a prayer slot from cathedral-orthodox lists, or growing intolerant in ways that strain the expedition's morale.

### Open Questions for Anders

1. The Hammer's working name — Forge-Sworn, Ofenheim's Hammer, The Twin-Tailed Anvil, or something he'd prefer?
2. Iconography: pure Twin-Tailed Comet Sigmarite, or Ofenstadt-furnace symbology layered on top (sparks, anvils, bellows as icons of faith)?
3. Hubris or pure conviction? My sketch has Rostbach as steady iron; could equally be sketched as a man whose conviction occasionally outruns wisdom. Affects flavour more than mechanics.
