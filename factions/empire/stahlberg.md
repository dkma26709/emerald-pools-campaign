## Background

A career soldier who has spent more years under arms than most men in Ofenstadt have spent alive. Old, grey, and hard as the iron the city produces. Speaks little, wastes nothing, and has forgotten more about keeping men alive on the march than most captains ever learn. The soldiers follow him not out of love but out of the simple understanding that listening to Stahlberg is what keeps you alive.

## Concept — Direction Sketch

*First-pass design direction, pending Anders feedback. Not committed mechanics.*

### Identity

Werner Stahlberg is the practical counterweight to Rostbach's faith. He's not inspiring — he's *correct*. The men follow him because listening to Stahlberg is what keeps them alive, and after a few weeks on the march that becomes its own kind of devotion. He's old, weathered, and economical. Every action is necessary; every word is short. Where Rostbach is iron faith, Stahlberg is iron *system*: drill, formation, banners, orders, the Empire's military tradition compressed into one grey old man who's seen most of it. The Iron Standard he carries isn't important because of the cloth — it's important because Stahlberg is at the bottom of it. The banner just lets people see him from far away.

Tone is **cold and weary** — economical and hard, with the weight of every man who didn't come back behind every decision. Stahlberg and Rostbach are **peers**, not teacher and student; competence respecting competence.

### Naming Palette

German military terminology + steel/iron palette + banner-craft. *Standarte*, *Fahne* (flag), *Disziplin*, *Befehl* (order), *Drill*, *Marsch*, *Wache* (watch), *Hauptmann* (captain). His name itself is a thesis — *Stahl* (steel) + *berg* (mountain). Track tiers can read as drill stages — Stand → Hold → Form → March → Charge — or watch rotations. Item names lean to **The Iron Standard**, **The Ofenstadt Banner**, **The Marshal's Pennant**, **The Captain's Watch**, **Stahl-Sworn**.

### Proposed Tracks (4)

1. **The Iron Standard (5 tiers)** — *the banner he carries*
   The army's BSB track, but bigger than that — the banner *is* Stahlberg's identity. An old standard, weathered by more campaigns than anyone alive can name, gaining weight as the expedition survives. Themes: army-wide morale, anti-rout, panic re-rolls, formation bonuses, a "the line did not break" intervention once per battle. Higher tiers extend the BSB bubble or grant the standard its own protection (last-stand effects, stay-on-table-at-zero-wounds for the bearer).

2. **The Repeater (3 tiers)** — *his personal weapon*
   He picks his shots. The Repeater Handgun is unusual for a Captain and that's the character of the track — Stahlberg shoots characters, war machines, monsters, never anonymous infantry. Themes: anti-character marksmanship, BS bonuses, range increases, single-decisive-shot once-per-battle (sparingly), at higher tier upgrade-toward-Hochland-rifle-style precision (target-character-in-unit). Smaller of his two weapon-tracks; The Banner is the headline.

3. **The Old Soldier (3 levels)** — *forty years under arms*
   What Stahlberg's experience does to the army around him on the table. Themes: deny ambush/scout against his army, "Stahlberg saw it coming" once-per-battle reaction, deployment vanguard, march-blocker resistance, reform-as-free-action, perhaps choose-redeploy on a single unit pre-game. The *grim, practical, the situation is under control* track. Fewer flashy combat effects; more "the wheels stay on."

4. **Ascension: General of the Empire (3 tiers)** — Captain → General
   Standard shape. Tier 1: +1 W or +1 Ld. Tier 2: extended Inspiring Presence, or army-wide reform-as-free-action. Tier 3: Lord choice — General of the Empire, full Lord-tier characteristic upgrade, possibly a one-shot army-wide rally.

### The Witch Hunter Weave (Light Touch)

Rostbach carries the heavy weave. Stahlberg's *starting* tier on **The Iron Standard** flexes with the Witch Hunter outcome:

- **Inquisitor's Brief** → the army arrived prepped. Stahlberg starts the Standard's first tier already unlocked.
- **Inquisitor's Warning** → starts standard.
- **Missing Agent** → starts standard, but the army carries a small permanent anxiety modifier (perhaps a -1 Ld in the first scenario as the column adjusts to operating blind), removed once Stahlberg earns the Standard's first tier.

Light enough that Stahlberg's design isn't *about* the Witch Hunter, but the connection is there.
