## Background

An apprentice of the College of Steel and the youngest of the expedition's leaders. The College sent him — more accurately, did not stop him from going. For Giest the journey south is as much about proving himself to his masters as it is about finding a missing merchant. Clever, eager, and not yet wise enough to know the true dangers of his craft.

## Concept — Direction Sketch

*First-pass design direction, pending Anders feedback. Not committed mechanics.*

### Identity

Theodor Giest is the third leg of the table — and the unbalanced one. Where Rostbach is iron faith and Stahlberg is iron system, Giest is iron *transmuted*: alchemy, the College of Steel's craft, base metal turned into something else. He's young, clever, and not yet bruised. The College didn't send him so much as *fail to stop him*, which Giest hears as endorsement and Rostbach hears as warning. He has something to prove, and the worry is what he'll prove it with. The Lore of Metal is a discipline that rewards confidence and punishes overreach, and Giest hasn't found the line yet. The expedition is his thesis.

There's a natural sympathy with Ofenstadt that the locals don't quite see — his magic and the city's industry both work in metal, and Giest finds the furnaces fascinating in a way that occasionally annoys Stahlberg, who has been near furnaces his whole life and considers them work, not poetry.

### Naming Palette

Alchemical + Gold Order + German wizardry. *Magister*, *Lehrling* (apprentice), *Geselle* (journeyman), *Goldener Orden*, *Stahlrod* (steel rod), *Auric*, *Argent*, *Quicksilver*, *Crucible*, *Alembic*. Track tiers can read as alchemical stages — Calcination → Dissolution → Tempering → Distillation → Transmutation. Item names lean to **The Auric Conductor**, **Gilt Lining**, **The Chymist's Staff**, **Aurum-Sworn**, **The Apprentice's Crucible**.

### Proposed Tracks (4)

1. **The Conductor (5 tiers)** — *his alchemical staff*
   Starts as an apprentice's instrument, becomes a Magister's. Themes: casting and channelling improvements, dispel re-rolls, signature-spell empowerment, miscast mitigation. Higher tiers grant access to *unique* Metal spells — Ofenstadt-flavoured workings the College's standard Lore doesn't include (e.g., a forge-themed augment, an anti-undead transmutation reflecting the dark energy thread). Naming reads as alchemical refinement.

2. **The Gilt Lining (3 tiers)** — *the Gold Order's robes*
   The College of Steel's gold-trimmed armoured robes. Glittering Robe is his signature spell, and the robes are its physical counterpart. Themes: armour or ward saves, Magic Resistance, anti-undead protection at higher tiers (Death lore feels different to a Gold wizard — like watching counterfeit metal). Smaller of his two equipment tracks; fewer fireworks, more survivability.

3. **The Apprentice (3 levels)** — *the path from Lehrling to Magister*
   Giest's growth as a wizard. Themes: gaining additional Metal spells (he starts knowing three, can earn the rest of the Lore), spell-selection improvements (pick rather than roll at higher level), a one-per-battle "the apprentice gets it right" effect — re-roll a failed cast, or convert the worst Miscast results.

   *Optional thread (flag for Anders):* the Level 1 tier could carry a one-per-battle compulsory channelling attempt representing Giest *trying too hard* — hubris-as-mechanic that later levels remove. Adds character but reduces fun-to-play.

4. **Ascension: Magister of the Gold Order (3 tiers)** — Battle Wizard → Magister Patriarch
   Standard shape. Tier 1: +1 W or Wizard Lv 2 → 3. Tier 2: full Lore access / Loremaster (Metal). Tier 3: Lord choice — Magister Patriarch, Wizard Lv 4, possibly a unique signature working.

### The Witch Hunter Weave (Thematic Touch)

Giest and the Witch Hunter are mirrors — both young men sent on missions their institutions only half-endorse. The mechanical weave is light and sits on **The Conductor**:

- **Inquisitor's Brief** → unlikely intellectual allies. The Conductor gains a single tier whose unlock requires the Witch Hunter's continued survival in the field. They feed each other information across institutional lines.
- **Inquisitor's Warning** → fragments. Giest can study what was recovered. Tier still earnable but harder.
- **Missing Agent** → Giest is the only one who reads what the Witch Hunter would have read. Tier remains earnable through his own investigations, but slower and blind. Pairs with Rostbach inheriting the Inquisitor's Mantle — the priest takes the *role*, the wizard takes the *research*.

**Quiet tension to note (no mechanic, just colour):** Witch Hunters historically dislike wizards. If the Inquisitor survives, Giest has to navigate that. Possible Sub-Objective hook — does Giest earn the Inquisitor's tolerance, or merely tolerate the suspicion? Rostbach probably ends up brokering peace.

### Open Questions for Anders

1. **Equipment beyond hand weapon?** Familiar, scroll, power stone, relic from his master? Affects whether *The Conductor* track grows from a starting item or builds one from nothing.
2. **Signature spell?** Glittering Robe currently. Mechanically defensive; Searing Doom (or similar) would shift the identity to offensive. Affects whether *The Conductor* leans defensive or offensive at higher tiers.
3. **Hubris-as-mechanic?** Yes (Level 1 carries a cost that progression removes), or no (clean upside-only progression)?
4. **A specific master at the College?** Narrative hook — letters home, an unfinished thesis, a specimen expected back. Threads for sub-objectives.
